# Diagnosis

## What Story A Is Doing Very Well

### 1. Voice

“I was but a boy when I left home…” 

The confessional tone works. Harald feels immediate and embodied.

### 2. Source Juxtaposition

Alternating Heimskringla with Harald commentary is conceptually strong. It:

* Creates tension between legend and lived experience.
* Shows myth formation in real time.

### 3. Pagan Conversion Moment

The line:

> “I pledged my troth to the old Gods, chose Odin as my lord…” 

That’s powerful. Clean. Defining.

---

## What Is Not Working in Story A

### 1. Tonal Modernity

“Monkey suit”
“It was a hell of a party.”

These collapse the historical illusion.

### 2. Erotic Seeress Scene

The line:

> “When I finished on her chest…”

This reads more adolescent than mythic. It undercuts gravitas.

### 3. Structural Stop-Start

Long saga blocks slow momentum and feel academically inserted.

---

## What Story B Is Doing Very Well

### 1. Narrative Cohesion

It reads like a novel, not a project.

### 2. Thematic Spine

Exile = forging of king.

That is strong architecture.

### 3. Seeress Scene

Symbolic intimacy without crude literalism. This is vastly more powerful.

### 4. Battle Writing

The description of Stiklestad balances chaos and clarity.

---

## What Is Not Working in Story B

### 1. Slight Emotional Distance

We don’t feel Harald’s interiority as sharply as in Story A.

### 2. Slight Safety

It is excellent — but rarely shocking.

---

**Story B is the stronger novel.**

## **Introduction**
The true story of Harald Harðráði: poet, adventurer, Captain of the Varangian Guard, King of Norway, Earl of Northumbria, and last of the Vikings.

BOOK MODE — CHAPTER 1 GENERATION PROMPT
Title: Chapter 1: 1030
Subtitle: Destiny Awaits
Length: 4000 words
Geography: Ringarike → Oslo → Trondheim → Stiklestad -> Uppsala -> Novgorod
Characters: Harald, Asta, Sigurd, Olaf, Voluspa
Sources: Heimskringla, Poetic Edda, Adam of Bremen
StoryArc: Act 1, exposition
Objectives:
  - In Ringarike, come of age
  - Travel to Trondheim and gain honor by fighting for Olaf at the Battle of Stiklestad
  - Travel to Uppsala and gain the blessings of Odin
  - Travel to Novgorod to gain the hospitality of Yaroslav
Story Beats:
 - The Boy Who Would Be King
  - Harald's family and background, foreshadow his destiny to be king.
 - Life on the Farm
  - Daily life in Ringarike. Farming, animal husbandry, pastoral, hunting, crafts. Family structures. Social structures in a farming village.
 - Battle of Stiklestad
  - War-arrow arrived calling men to fight for Olaf. Journey from Oslo to Trondheim. Battle scene, Olaf slain, Harald wounded, escapes with core band to foothills, retreat into forests.
 - Message of the Runes
  - Pagan conversion moment, Harald commits to Odin. Winter in Swedish uplands: skis, sleds, frozen rivers, hunger, men desert slowly.
 - Ash tree in Uppsala
  - Uppsala Freyr/Freyja fertility revels, blood sacrifice. Voluspa, Odin seeress Norn, receives Harald's prayers to Odin, sees his destiny, and pledges to be his spritual/sexual partner in his pagan religious journey/natural life. 
Tropes:
- Warrior childhood prophecy
- Viking battles
- Pagan vs. Christian cosmology
- Sacred groves and human sacrifice
- Exile as crucible
- Sex + prophecy linkage
- Odin worship
- Uppsala temple
- Ash tree
- Bloody sacrificial rites
- Seeress prophecy
- Drinking, feasting
